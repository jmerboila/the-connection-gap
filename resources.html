Resources

Keep following the signal.

A curated, verified shortlist — the strongest data, theory, reporting, grassroots projects, and course-aligned readings on the digital divide. Sorted so you can start anywhere.

How this was curated

These sources are chosen and ordered deliberately. Start with the data that establishes the gap, move to the course-aligned readings that frame it in theory, then to field reporting that gives it a human face, and finally to projects already closing it. Curation isn't collecting links — it's sequencing them so a newcomer can build understanding one step at a time.

Data & reports +
Digital 2025: The Philippines datareportal.com

The annual snapshot of internet, mobile, and social-media use — the source for headline penetration figures and who remains offline.

 Unlocking the Philippines' Digital Transformation worldbank.org

Where the "widening gap" data lives: broadband by household, the wealth-quintile divide, and the tower build-out.

 Internet usage in the Philippines statista.com

A statistics hub for trend lines on penetration, mobile-first behaviour, and the digital economy.
Course-aligned readings +
Understanding the Digital Divide (OECD) oecd-ilibrary.org

The foundational text that frames the divide around income and education — the origin of the access framework this project builds on.

 The Impact of Digital Technologies (UN) un.org

A concise overview of how connectivity shapes development, inequality, and rights — the social-impact lens at a global scale.

 Digital Media Trends 2024 (Deloitte) deloitte.com

On convergence, creators, and generative AI — context for emerging technologies and content curation.

 Visions of the Internet in 2035 (Pew) pewresearch.org

Expert forecasts on digital rights, civic engagement, and accessibility — the future-of-media frame for closing the gap.
Theory & academic +
Voices of Philippine University Students (COVID-19) eric.ed.gov · PDF

A peer-reviewed look at how remote learning exposed and deepened inequality among Filipino students.

 Contributing Factors on Poor Digital Literacy rsisinternational.org

A review of why the second-level (skills) divide persists even as access expands.

 Flexible Online Learning in Rural Areas ijlter.org

Field evidence from rural students relying on mobile data and patchy signal — the access divide, documented.
Reporting & voices +
The Crisis Facing Philippine Schoolchildren time.com

Long-form reporting on one of the world's longest school closures and the families caught in it.

 Distance Learning Reveals the Digital Divide boell.org

On-the-ground accounts plus the PISA context that frames the learning crisis.

 Online Classes & Digital Rights engagemedia.org

Frames connectivity as a rights issue and tracks how universities scrambled to keep students enrolled.
Projects & action +
The eLibrary "Library-in-a-Box" Project philstarlife.com

An offline-first answer to the divide — and the closest kin to DigiSkills PH's own approach.

 Closing the Distance to Digital Access bworldonline.com

A clear primer on why the archipelago makes infrastructure hard — and what's being built to fix it.
