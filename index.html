DigiSkills PH

Digital skills that work offline.

Millions of Filipino children are growing up on the wrong side of the connection gap. DigiSkills PH is a free, offline-first app that brings digital-literacy and online-safety lessons to low-end phones — no data plan required.

The solution

An app built for the side without signal.

Most digital-learning tools assume fast, always-on internet and a capable device. DigiSkills PH assumes the opposite — and turns that constraint into its design.

Offline-first

Lessons live on the phone

Content is stored on the device and works with no connection. When a signal appears, progress syncs quietly in the background — emerging tech serving access, not assuming it.

Low-end ready

Made for cheap phones

Lightweight by design, so it runs on the entry-level Android devices most families actually own. Accessibility is the feature, not an afterthought.

Curated

Short, sequenced micro-lessons

Each skill is curated and ordered into bite-sized steps a child can finish on a borrowed phone in minutes — content curation in service of learning, not engagement.

Safety first

Starts with staying safe online

Phase 1 teaches the digital-citizenship basics — privacy, scams, cyberbullying, and spotting misinformation — before a child is ever exposed to the open web.

The roadmap

One app, built in phases.

DigiSkills PH grows from a focused safety primer into a full low-bandwidth learning tool — each phase shipping only what a low-connectivity household can actually use.

Phase 1 · Now

Stay Safe Online

A complete offline course in online safety and digital citizenship: passwords and privacy, recognising scams and misinformation, kind and respectful communication, and managing a digital footprint.

Phase 2 · Next

Core digital literacy

Foundational skills — searching, evaluating sources, basic productivity — that move a learner from the access divide into genuine, usable capability.

Phase 3 · Future

A low-bandwidth classroom

A full offline learning platform for children in low-connectivity homes — open educational resources delivered to the device, syncing whenever a signal allows.

Why this matters

Being online is now a precondition for everything else.

Digital media is the default infrastructure for school, work, money, and belonging. When a child is missing from it, they are not merely inconvenienced — they are structurally excluded from the modern economy and public life.

This site maps the gap DigiSkills PH is built to close. It examines the divide across three levels — access, skills, and outcomes — follows the gap into a real classroom crisis, and curates the resources to keep learning.
